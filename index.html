UAV with a Hand

A major challenge when using supervised learning techniques is the scarcity of labeled data. The problem arises due to the significant amount of human work hours required to perform the labeling and also in some cases the necessity of experts (eg: labeling bio-medical images) to perform the task. As a solution to this problem active learning approaches have been used to support the human annotator. In this project we explore different variations of active learning methods which intelligently select data points from the large unlabeled dataset and use the small labeled dataset to train classifiers for detecting tumorous areas in MRI images of patients with brain tumor. Mainly three sampling methods, namely Least confident sampling, Margin sampling and Entropy sampling are evaluated. Along with them three uncertainty measures are used. They are Feature uncertainty, Geometric uncertainty and Joint uncertainty. Joint uncertainty is a newly proposed measure of uncertainty that captures the difference in feature and geometric probabilities. Out of all the methods evaluated, Margin sampling with feature uncertainty gives the best results. By just using 2% of the complete training dataset, this method is capable of achieving ~96% of the dice score that is achieved when using the 100% of the dataset.
